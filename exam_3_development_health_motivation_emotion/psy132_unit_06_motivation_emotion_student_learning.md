# Unit 6: Motivation & Emotion

## Topics in this unit

- **Motivation**
- **Emotion**

## Topic 17: Motivation (media + decision literacy)

1. **Motivation** is what energizes and directs behavior, and it includes both *internal states* (needs, goals, values) and *external forces* (rewards, pressures, opportunities).
2. **Biological drives** support survival by pushing behavior toward **homeostasis**, but many modern motivations are driven by *learned incentives* and *social meaning*.
3. **Intrinsic motivation** tends to be stronger when people experience **autonomy**, **competence**, and **connection**, while **extrinsic motivation** can help behavior start but can also *crowd out interest* if handled poorly.
4. **Goals** shape attention and persistence, so **specific plans** and **feedback** usually outperform vague intentions, especially when obstacles and time limits are predictable.
5. Motivation is not constant across situations, so behavior is often best explained by the interaction of **person**, **task**, and **environment** rather than by *lazy versus driven* labels.
6. Many motivation claims can be tested by asking what the behavior is **rewarded** by or what it helps the person *avoid*, and what *costs* or *barriers* are silently shaping choices.

## Topic 18: Emotion (media + decision literacy)

1. **Emotions** are coordinated patterns involving **physiological arousal**, **subjective experience**, **expressive behavior**, and **action tendencies** that prepare us to respond to important events.
2. Emotion theories differ, but a useful shared idea is that the brain combines **body signals** with *interpretation* of the situation to produce the experience you call a **feeling**.
3. **Appraisal** matters, meaning the same event can lead to different emotions depending on what it is judged to mean for **goals**, **identity**, **safety**, and **control**.
4. **Emotion regulation** strategies like **reappraisal**, **problem solving**, and **avoidance** can reduce distress, but the best strategy depends on **context** and *long-term costs*.
5. Emotions influence **attention** and **memory**, which is why strong feelings can make some details vivid while *distorting timelines*, causes, or other people’s intentions.
6. When evaluating claims about emotional control or **emotional intelligence**, look for evidence that separates **skill** from **personality**, includes measurable outcomes, and accounts for **culture** and situation.

## Unit-level six sentences

## Unit 6: Motivation & Emotion (media + decision literacy)

1. **Motivation** directs behavior through **needs**, **goals**, incentives, and meaning, so actions often make sense when you identify what they are **rewarding** or **relieving**.
2. **Intrinsic motivation** grows with **autonomy**, **competence**, and **connection**, while **extrinsic rewards** can help short-term behavior but sometimes *undermine interest*.
3. Habits and **environments** often drive motivation more than *willpower*, which is why changing **cues** and *friction points* can matter more than trying harder.
4. **Emotions** are coordinated response patterns involving **body signals**, *interpretation*, and **action tendencies**, so feelings are informative but not automatic verdicts.
5. **Appraisal** and **context** shape emotion, which is why the same situation can trigger different reactions across people or across time in the same person.
6. **Emotion regulation** is not suppression; it is choosing strategies like **reappraisal**, **problem solving**, or *seeking support* that fit the situation and reduce *long-term cost*.
