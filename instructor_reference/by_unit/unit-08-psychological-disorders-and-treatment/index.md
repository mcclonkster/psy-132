# Unit 8: Psychological Disorders and Treatment

<!-- GENERATED:UNIT-BRIEF-START -->
## Unit study brief

### Big ideas
- Disorders are defined by distress and impairment, not weirdness.
- Diagnosis is a tool for communication and treatment planning, not an identity.
- Multiple models explain disorders; avoid single-cause overreach.
- Treatments include psychotherapy and biomedical approaches with evidence limits.
- Stigma and ethics matter: framing affects harm and help-seeking.

### Common traps
- Using labels as identities or insults.
- Assuming a single cause explains a disorder.
- Confusing normal stress with disorder-level impairment automatically.
- Assuming treatment is one-size-fits-all.
- Believing myths about schizophrenia and violence.

### Key terms
- **Diagnosis**: Classification label describing a pattern to guide care.
- **DSM**: Manual providing diagnostic criteria.
- **Comorbidity**: Co-occurrence of two or more disorders.
- **Diathesis-stress model**: Vulnerability plus stressors can produce disorders.
- **Psychotherapy**: Treatment using psychological methods (e.g., CBT).
- **Medication**: Biomedical treatment affecting symptoms via biological mechanisms.
- **Stigma**: Social devaluation linked to a label or trait.
- **Prevalence**: How common a disorder is in a population.
- **Evidence-based treatment**: Treatment supported by systematic research evidence.
- **Impairment**: Reduced ability to function in important life areas.
