### Common misconceptions

- One therapy works best for all problems and all people.
- If a treatment works in a study, it will work the same way for everyone in real life.
- Common factors mean technique never matters.
- Therapeutic alliance is just being nice and does not affect outcomes.
- Psychotherapy is only talking and does not involve skill change or practice.
- Medication always fixes the underlying cause.
- Medication is the only effective treatment for serious problems.
- Placebo effects mean improvement is fake.
- If symptoms improve, functioning must also improve.
- If a person does not improve, they are not trying hard enough.
- Therapies can be compared fairly without considering severity, comorbidity, preference, and access.
