## PSY-132 Topic 14: Language

### Speech sounds and articulation (click, hear, and see how sounds are made)
- Sounds of Speech (University of Iowa): https://soundsofspeech.uiowa.edu/
- Seeing Speech (interactive IPA charts with MRI/ultrasound/animation): https://www.seeingspeech.ac.uk/ipa-charts/
- International Phonetic Association (official interactive clickable IPA chart): https://www.internationalphoneticassociation.org/IPAcharts/IPA_charts_TI/IPA_charts_TI.html
- Interactive IPA Chart (simple click-to-hear): https://www.ipachart.com/
- Interactive American IPA Chart (General American; click symbols): https://americanipachart.com/
- Sounds American (interactive American English sound chart): https://soundsamerican.net/
- Color Vowel Chart (click colors to hear vowel sounds): https://americanenglish.state.gov/resources/color-vowel-chart

### Speech perception illusions and categories (very fast, very memorable)
- The McGurk Effect (interactive “click the video to start” demo): https://speechneurolab.ca/en/leffet-mcgurk/
- Categorical Perception demo (identify /ba/–/da/ and /ra/–/la/ continua; includes trial links): https://splab.net/apd/u100/

### Grammar rules you “know” without being taught (classic Wug Test activities)
- The Wug Test – Part 1 (plural endings): https://sites.psu.edu/linguisticexperiments/wugtestpart1/
- The Wug Test – Part 2 (past tense endings): https://sites.psu.edu/linguisticexperiments/wugtestpart2/

### Word recognition and lexical access (reaction time tasks)
- Lexical Decision Task (PsyToolkit): https://www.psytoolkit.org/experiment-library/ldt.html
- Example Lexical Decision Task (in-browser, runs locally): https://waltervanheuven.net/teaching/examples-exp-tasks/LDT/

### Learning words from a continuous stream (statistical learning mini-experiment)
- Parachuting Into Language (listen + test sheet): https://sites.psu.edu/linguisticexperiments/parachuting-into-language/

### Meaning in context (pragmatics, using memes)
- The Role of the Unspoken (task sheet): https://sites.psu.edu/linguisticexperiments/the-role-of-the-unspoken/

### Dialects and accents (interactive maps you can explore)
- Dynamic Dialects (interactive accent map and chart): https://www.dynamicdialects.ac.uk/accent-map/
- Our Dialects (30+ interactive dialect maps): https://www.ourdialects.uk/
- Localingual (click a country/region to hear voices): https://www.localingual.com/

### Optional: Sound-to-spelling challenge (IPA-focused)
- Spelling Based on Sound (IPA activity): https://sites.psu.edu/linguisticexperiments/spelling-based-on-sound/
