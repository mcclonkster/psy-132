### Common misconceptions

- Sensation and perception are the same thing.
- Transduction means interpretation rather than energy-to-signal conversion.
- Thresholds are fixed personal limits that do not change with context.
- If someone fails to detect a stimulus, the sensory system is broken.
- Weber’s law means the same absolute change should be equally noticeable at all intensities.
- Sensory adaptation means the stimulus disappears rather than responsiveness changes.
- Rods and cones do the same job in vision.
- Frequency and amplitude in sound both describe loudness.
- Place theory and timing theory are competing and one must be correct in all cases.
- Pain is identical to nociception, so pain always reflects tissue damage level.
- If pain is influenced by context, it is not real.

## Big ideas

- Sensation as detection and conversion of physical energy
- Transduction as the conversion step into neural signals
- Thresholds as limits on detection and discrimination
- Adaptation as reduced sensitivity over time to constant input
- Sensation versus perception as detection versus interpretation
## Common misconceptions

- Sensation and perception are the same
- Transduction means translating into words
- Stronger sensation always means more accurate experience
- Senses record reality perfectly
- If you miss a stimulus, you must not have been paying attention
## Key terms

- **Sensation**: The detection of physical energy by sensory receptors.
- **Transduction**: Converting physical energy into neural signals.
- **Sensory receptor**: A cell that responds to a specific kind of energy, like light or pressure.
- **Absolute threshold**: The minimum intensity needed to detect a stimulus some of the time.
- **Difference threshold**: The smallest change in a stimulus that can be detected, also called just-noticeable difference.
- **Signal detection**: A framework for understanding detection that includes attention, expectation, and decision criteria.
- **Sensory adaptation**: Reduced sensitivity after constant exposure to a stimulus.
- **Modality**: A sensory system, such as vision or hearing.
- **Wavelength**: A physical property of light related to color perception.
- **Frequency**: A physical property of sound related to pitch perception.
