### Common misconceptions

- Consciousness can be defined by one simple measure, such as EEG alone.
- Dreaming only occurs during REM sleep, so REM equals dreaming.
- Sleep is wasted time and has no important cognitive function.
- People can adapt to chronic sleep deprivation without meaningful performance costs.
- Depressant means sadness rather than reduced central nervous system activation.
- Psychoactive substances have no effects beyond choice or personality.
- Drug category labels guarantee one simple effect for everyone.
- Tolerance, dependence, and addiction are the same thing.
- Hypnosis is mind control or reveals hidden truth.
- Meditation guarantees dramatic mental health changes for everyone.
- Altered states are inherently irrational or inherently insightful.

## Big ideas

- Sleep as an active brain state with distinct stages
- Circadian rhythms as a timing system for alertness and sleep
- Sleep cycles as predictable patterns across the night
- Sleep loss as a measurable hit to attention, memory, and mood
- Dream claims as a place to practice evidence limits
## Common misconceptions

- Sleep is brain off
- You can fully catch up on sleep with one long weekend night
- Dreams always have one hidden meaning
- Being tired just means you need more willpower
- Caffeine affects everyone the same way at any time
## Key terms

- **Consciousness**: Awareness of yourself and your environment.
- **Circadian rhythm**: A roughly 24-hour biological timing cycle that affects sleep and alertness.
- **REM sleep**: A sleep stage linked to rapid eye movement and many vivid dream reports.
- **Non-REM sleep**: Sleep stages that are not REM, including deeper slow-wave sleep.
- **Sleep cycle**: A repeating pattern of non-REM and REM stages during the night.
- **Sleep deprivation**: Not getting enough sleep to support normal functioning.
- **Insomnia**: Difficulty falling asleep, staying asleep, or getting restorative sleep.
- **Sleep apnea**: A disorder involving breathing interruptions during sleep.
- **REM rebound**: An increase in REM sleep after a period of REM deprivation.
- **Lucid dreaming**: A dream in which the dreamer is aware they are dreaming.
