### Common misconceptions

- Perception equals reality.
- Bottom-up processing is always truth and top-down processing is always bias.
- Illusions mean perception is weak or defective rather than showing useful assumptions and limits.
- Attention is stable and continuous, so people should always notice obvious events.
- Multitasking means doing multiple tasks at full quality without costs.
- Inattentional blindness means a person does not care or is unintelligent.
- Change blindness only happens in lab tasks and not in real life.
- Signal detection differences are only motivation, not a combination of sensitivity and criterion.
- Binocular cues are always required for depth perception.
- Multisensory effects mean one sense overrides another in all situations.
- A single Gestalt principle explains all organization, rather than multiple tendencies operating together.

## Big ideas

- Perception as active interpretation, not passive recording
- Bottom-up and top-down processing working together
- Context and expectations shaping what is perceived
- Perceptual organization as rules for grouping and meaning
- Illusions as evidence of predictable perception shortcuts
## Common misconceptions

- Perception is passive copying of the world
- Top-down processing means making things up
- If perception can be wrong, it is useless
- Attention means you notice everything important
- Illusions only happen to certain people
## Key terms

- **Perception**: The process of organizing and interpreting sensory input.
- **Bottom-up processing**: Building perception from sensory details coming in.
- **Top-down processing**: Using prior knowledge and expectations to interpret input.
- **Perceptual set**: A readiness to perceive something in a particular way based on context and expectations.
- **Selective attention**: Focusing on some information while filtering out other information.
- **Gestalt principles**: Rules the brain uses to group elements into meaningful patterns.
- **Figure-ground**: Separating an object from its background.
- **Perceptual constancy**: Perceiving objects as stable despite changes in lighting, distance, or angle.
- **Depth perception**: Judging distance and three-dimensional space.
- **Illusion**: A mismatch between physical reality and what is perceived.
