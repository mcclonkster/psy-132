# Unit 2: Biology of Behavior and Experience

<!-- GENERATED:UNIT-BRIEF-START -->
## Unit study brief

### Big ideas
- Neural communication uses electrical signaling plus chemical transmission at synapses.
- Sensation detects physical energy; perception interprets it into meaning.
- Thresholds and signal detection explain why detection is imperfect and context-sensitive.
- Perception uses shortcuts; illusions show predictable tendencies, not ‘bad eyes.’
- Sleep is an active, staged brain process that affects attention and learning.

### Common traps
- Treating brain activity differences as proof of causation.
- Collapsing sensation and perception into one process.
- Assuming thresholds are fixed and purely sensory.
- Treating top-down processing as “making things up.”
- Treating sleep as “brain off.”

### Key terms
- **Neuron**: Cell that sends and receives signals.
- **Synapse**: Junction where neurons communicate.
- **Action potential**: Electrical signal traveling down a neuron.
- **Neurotransmitter**: Chemical messenger released at synapses.
- **Transduction**: Converting physical energy into neural signals.
- **Absolute threshold**: Minimum intensity needed to detect a stimulus some of the time.
- **Difference threshold**: Smallest detectable change in a stimulus.
- **Bottom-up processing**: Perception built from sensory input.
- **Top-down processing**: Perception shaped by expectations and knowledge.
- **REM sleep**: Sleep stage linked to vivid dream reports and distinct physiology.

### Key studies
- **Stroop effect**: Automatic reading interferes with color naming, revealing attention limits. | Why it matters: Links perception/attention to measurable performance.
- **Hubel & Wiesel visual cortex work**: Visual neurons respond to specific features, showing organized processing. | Why it matters: Connects sensation/perception to neural coding.
