### Common misconceptions

- One brain region fully explains a complex behavior or mental process.
- Brain images show thoughts directly rather than proxies measured under assumptions.
- fMRI measures neural activity directly.
- Correlation in brain data proves causation.
- Neurotransmitters have one simple effect, such as dopamine equals pleasure or serotonin equals happiness.
- Chemical imbalance is a complete explanation for behavior or mental health.
- Left-brain versus right-brain types explain personality and intelligence.
- Autonomic nervous system is an on-off switch between sympathetic and parasympathetic states.
- Neuroplasticity means the brain can become anything instantly with enough willpower.
- Neuroscience explanations make psychological and social explanations unnecessary.

## Big ideas

- Neurons as the basic communication units of the nervous system
- Brain systems working in networks, not isolated parts
- Plasticity as change in the brain across experience
- Limits of brain-based evidence for strong causal claims
- Multiple levels of explanation for behavior, not brain-only answers
## Common misconceptions

- One brain part causes complex behavior by itself
- More brain activity always means better performance
- Brain scans prove a cause automatically
- Left-brain and right-brain people are fixed types
- A brain explanation replaces a psychological explanation
## Key terms

- **Neuron**: A nerve cell that receives, processes, and sends signals.
- **Synapse**: The junction where one neuron communicates with another.
- **Neurotransmitter**: A chemical messenger that carries a signal across a synapse.
- **Action potential**: An electrical signal that travels down a neuron.
- **Plasticity**: The brain’s ability to change structure and function with experience.
- **Central nervous system**: The brain and spinal cord.
- **Peripheral nervous system**: The nerves that connect the body to the brain and spinal cord.
- **Cerebral cortex**: The outer layer of the brain involved in many higher functions.
- **Hippocampus**: A brain structure important for forming new explicit memories.
- **Prefrontal cortex**: A region involved in planning, self-control, and decision making.
