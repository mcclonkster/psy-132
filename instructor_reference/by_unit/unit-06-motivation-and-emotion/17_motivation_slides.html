Biological Regulation

Homeostasis & Drive

Our bodies work like a thermostat to maintain internal stability.

Drive Theory: Physiological needs create psychological "pushes" to act.

"Motivation is the body's way of telling the mind to fix a survival-related problem."

The Drive-Reduction Cycle

Need

Physiological deficit (e.g., low blood sugar)

Drive

Internal tension (Feeling of hunger)

Behavior

Goal-directed action (Eating a snack)

Satiation

Homeostasis restored, drive reduced

The Hierarchy of Needs

Maslow organized human motives into a pyramid, suggesting we prioritize survival before self-fulfillment.

  • Basic: Food, Shelter, Safety
  • Social: Belonging & Esteem
  • Growth: Self-Actualization

Social Motives

Achievement

The drive to succeed, master skills, and overcome obstacles. Linked to high persistence.

Affiliation

The need for social connection and being part of a group. Crucial for evolutionary survival.

Intimacy

The desire for close, personal, and emotionally deep relationships with specific others.
