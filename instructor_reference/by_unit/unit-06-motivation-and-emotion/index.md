# Unit 6: Motivation and Emotion

<!-- GENERATED:UNIT-BRIEF-START -->
## Unit study brief

### Big ideas
- Motivation is shaped by needs, incentives, goals, and meaning.
- Intrinsic vs extrinsic motivation affects persistence and performance.
- Emotion includes arousal plus interpretation and action tendencies.
- Stress affects cognition and health; coping and regulation matter.
- Motivation and emotion shape decision making and relationships.

### Common traps
- Assuming rewards always increase motivation long-term.
- Confusing emotion with mood or behavior.
- Treating stress as purely psychological without body effects.
- Assuming emotions can be switched off instantly by willpower.
- Treating one theory as explaining all motivation.

### Key terms
- **Intrinsic motivation**: Doing an activity for interest or satisfaction.
- **Extrinsic motivation**: Doing an activity for external rewards or outcomes.
- **Drive**: Internal state motivating behavior to reduce a need.
- **Incentive**: External stimulus that motivates behavior.
- **Emotion regulation**: Processes shaping which emotions occur and how they’re expressed.
- **Coping**: Strategies used to manage stressors.
- **Appraisal**: Interpretation of a situation shaping emotional response.
- **Homeostasis**: Maintaining stable internal conditions.
- **Self-determination theory**: Motivation shaped by autonomy, competence, relatedness.
- **Two-factor theory**: Emotion = arousal + cognitive label.

### Key examples
- **Grades vs curiosity**: intrinsic/extrinsic trade-offs | Student move: Predict persistence under different incentives.
- **Reappraisal move**: interpretation changes emotion | Student move: Rewrite a scenario with a healthier interpretation.

### Key studies
- **Schachter-Singer two-factor theory**: Emotion depends on arousal plus cognitive labeling. | Why it matters: Links interpretation to emotional experience.
- **Yerkes–Dodson law (classic)**: Performance can peak at moderate arousal depending on task. | Why it matters: Simple model for stress-performance tradeoffs.

### Activities
- **Coping sort**: Sort coping examples into problem-focused vs emotion-focused and justify. | Evidence: Discrimination.
