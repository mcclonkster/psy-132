### Common misconceptions

- Emotions are only feelings and have no physiological or behavioral components.
- Emotions are only physiology and do not involve meaning or appraisal.
- One emotion theory is the correct final theory and replaces the others.
- Arousal uniquely identifies a specific emotion in all situations.
- Expression always reflects inner emotion and cannot be shaped by norms.
- Expressions are fully universal or fully culture-specific with no overlap.
- Suppression is the same as emotion regulation in general.
- Reappraisal is just pretending and cannot change emotion.
- Emotion regulation means removing all negative emotion.
- The amygdala is the emotion center and fully explains fear and emotion.
- If emotions influence judgment, people are irrational in all decisions.
