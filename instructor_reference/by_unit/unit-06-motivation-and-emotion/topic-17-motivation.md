### Common misconceptions

- Motivation is a fixed trait rather than a process influenced by context and goals.
- Motivation is the same as emotion.
- Extrinsic motivation is always bad and ruins learning in all cases.
- Rewards always help and never undermine intrinsic interest.
- Self-efficacy is global confidence that applies to all tasks equally.
- Self-control is pure willpower and context does not matter.
- People who lack motivation are choosing not to care.
- Arousal always improves performance.
- Approach motivation is good and avoidance motivation is bad in all cases.
- Needs alone explain behavior, so incentives and goals are unimportant.
- Cue-triggered wanting means a person is weak rather than responding to learned salience.
