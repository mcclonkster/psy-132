### Common misconceptions

- Intelligence is a single fixed quantity that people have in a set amount.
- An intelligence quotient score is a direct measure of a person’s overall worth or potential.
- A test score has meaning without a normative sample.
- Percentile ranks are equal-interval measures and can be averaged like points.
- High reliability means a test is valid for all uses.
- Validity is a permanent label rather than evidence for a specific use.
- General intelligence factor is a single brain organ or a single ability that explains everything.
- Fluid and crystallized intelligence are the same thing.
- Heritability means a trait is unchangeable and environment does not matter.
- Heritability applies to individuals rather than populations under conditions.
- Group differences have a single cause and justify conclusions about individuals.
