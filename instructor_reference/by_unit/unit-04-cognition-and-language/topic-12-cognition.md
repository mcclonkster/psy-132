### Common misconceptions

- Good thinking is always slow and deliberate, so fast thinking is always irrational.
- Heuristics are always bad and should never be used.
- Biases prove people are lazy or unintelligent rather than showing predictable patterns under uncertainty.
- If a decision is biased, the person is choosing to be biased on purpose.
- Logic and reasoning are automatic and do not require practice or attention.
- Multitasking means doing two tasks at full quality without costs.
- Confidence guarantees accuracy.
- A vivid example proves something is common.
- Anchors only influence weak thinkers and do not affect most people.
- Schemas and scripts are the same as stereotypes and are always harmful.
- Framing effects mean people are irrational in all decisions rather than context-sensitive.
