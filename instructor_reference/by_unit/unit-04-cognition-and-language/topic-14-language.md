### Common misconceptions

- Language is mostly memorization of words and phrases rather than rule-governed structure.
- Syntax and semantics are the same thing.
- Pragmatics is just politeness rather than meaning in context.
- Language development follows a fixed timetable with the same ages for all children.
- Child-directed speech is required in one specific form for normal development.
- Bilingual children are confused and delayed by learning two languages.
- One deprivation case proves a critical period in a simple, decisive way.
- Language acquisition device is a confirmed anatomical module rather than a hypothesis.
- Language determines thought in a strong and universal way.
- Adults cannot learn new languages in meaningful ways after childhood.
