### Common misconceptions

- A single study can prove a claim with certainty.
- Only experiments count as real science, so descriptive and correlational research are not scientific.
- Correlation implies causation.
- If two things are related, one must directly cause the other.
- Random sampling and random assignment are the same thing.
- Having a control group automatically eliminates confounds.
- A statistically significant result means the effect is large or important.
- Reliability and validity are the same thing.
- Self-report is useless because people lie, so surveys cannot produce scientific knowledge.
- Replication means repeating a study exactly once, and if it fails, the original finding is false.
- Bias is only intentional dishonesty rather than a predictable source of error that can occur without bad intent.

## Big ideas

- Operational definitions as the bridge from ideas to measurement
- Correlation as association, not proof of cause
- Experiments as the strongest design for causal claims
- Random sampling versus random assignment as different tools
- Confounds and alternative explanations as the default threat to conclusions
## Common misconceptions

- Correlation proves causation
- Random sampling and random assignment are the same
- An operational definition is a dictionary definition
- Anecdotes are the same as data
- If a study is published, it must be true in all cases
## Key terms

- **Variable**: Anything that can take different values, such as sleep hours or test scores.
- **Operational definition**: The specific way a variable is measured or manipulated in a study.
- **Independent variable**: The variable a researcher changes or assigns to test its effect.
- **Dependent variable**: The outcome a researcher measures.
- **Correlation**: A relationship between variables that does not establish cause and effect.
- **Experiment**: A study design that manipulates an independent variable and measures its effect on a dependent variable.
- **Random sampling**: Selecting participants so the sample represents the population.
- **Random assignment**: Assigning participants to groups by chance to reduce group differences.
- **Confound**: A factor that varies with the independent variable and could also influence the outcome.
- **Replication**: Repeating a study to see whether the result holds up again.
