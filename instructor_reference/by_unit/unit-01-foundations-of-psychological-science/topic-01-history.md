### Common misconceptions

- Psychology is mostly common sense rather than a scientific field.
- Science means absolute certainty, so psychological findings should be final and unchanging.
- Early schools were replaced one by one, so later approaches made earlier ones irrelevant.
- Structuralism and functionalism were the same because both studied the mind.
- Psychoanalytic theory is the modern scientific consensus about behavior.
- Behaviorism denies that thoughts and feelings exist.
- Cognitive psychology is just introspection with a new name.
- Gestalt psychology is only about visual tricks rather than principles of organization.
- Humanistic psychology is only motivational language rather than a framework with specific claims.
- Multicultural psychology is optional and only relevant to a few groups rather than a cross-cutting lens for generalization.

## Big ideas

- Psychology as a science of behavior and mental processes
- Scientific methods as a way to test claims, not confirm intuitions
- Historical roots shaping modern questions and methods
- Multiple levels of explanation, from brain to culture
- Applied psychology as a bridge from research to real-world problems
## Common misconceptions

- Psychology is just common sense
- One famous study can prove a broad claim about people
- Science means lab equipment rather than methods and evidence
- If something feels true, it is true
- Psychology is only therapy
## Key terms

- **Psychology**: The scientific study of behavior and mental processes.
- **Behavior**: Observable actions that can be measured.
- **Mental processes**: Internal experiences such as perception, memory, emotion, and thinking.
- **Empiricism**: Building knowledge from systematic observation and measurement.
- **Hypothesis**: A testable prediction about what will happen or how variables relate.
- **Theory**: An organized explanation that integrates evidence and generates predictions.
- **Structuralism**: An early approach focused on describing the basic elements of conscious experience.
- **Functionalism**: An early approach focused on what mental processes and behaviors do for adaptation.
- **Introspection**: A method that asks people to report their conscious experiences.
- **Applied psychology**: Using psychological science to address real-world problems.
