# Unit 5: Development and Health Across the Lifespan

<!-- GENERATED:UNIT-BRIEF-START -->
## Unit study brief

### Big ideas
- Development is lifelong across physical, cognitive, and psychosocial domains.
- Nature and nurture interact; contexts shape outcomes within constraints.
- Attachment and early relationships influence later functioning.
- Adolescence includes identity formation and shifting demands.
- Health behavior is shaped by biology, psychology, and social context.

### Common traps
- Thinking development stops after childhood.
- Assuming one “normal” path fits everyone.
- Treating developmental correlations as causation.
- Assuming early experience determines everything permanently.
- Treating health behavior as pure willpower.

### Key terms
- **Attachment**: Emotional bond that shapes security and relationships.
- **Temperament**: Early patterns of reactivity and regulation.
- **Identity**: Sense of self shaped by roles, values, and context.
- **Puberty**: Biological changes leading to reproductive maturity.
- **Risk factor**: Condition increasing likelihood of negative outcome.
- **Protective factor**: Condition reducing risk or buffering stress.
- **Cohort effect**: Differences due to historical context, not age alone.
- **Health behavior**: Actions affecting health outcomes (sleep, diet, exercise, etc.).
- **Stress**: Response to demands affecting body and mind.
- **Developmental domain**: Physical, cognitive, or psychosocial area of change.
