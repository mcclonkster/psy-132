### Common misconceptions

- Development is mostly genetics and environment does not matter.
- Development is mostly parenting and other influences do not matter.
- Stage theories describe universal fixed sequences with the same ages for everyone.
- If a milestone is late, development is automatically abnormal.
- Attachment style determines adult outcomes with certainty.
- Parenting style guarantees outcomes and can be judged without context.
- Adolescence is always a period of inevitable turmoil and rebellion.
- Risk-taking is caused by personality weakness rather than social incentives and context.
- Aging means inevitable severe decline for everyone.
- Cohort and culture do not matter, so one timeline is universal.
- Grief follows a fixed sequence of stages for all people.
