# Unit 7: Social Behavior and Identity

<!-- GENERATED:UNIT-BRIEF-START -->
## Unit study brief

### Big ideas
- Attribution is interpretation; biases shape judgments and conflict.
- Social influence includes conformity, obedience, and persuasion.
- Groups can amplify errors (groupthink/polarization) or improve decisions with safeguards.
- Identity and culture shape behavior and experience.
- Prejudice and discrimination are attitudes vs behavior; contexts matter.

### Common traps
- Fundamental attribution error: overblaming traits, underweighting situation.
- Assuming confidence equals accuracy.
- Thinking conformity means weakness rather than context-driven behavior.
- Treating culture as background rather than a causal context.
- Assuming group decisions are automatically better.

### Key terms
- **Attribution**: Explanation for behavior.
- **Fundamental attribution error**: Overemphasizing traits and underemphasizing situations when judging others.
- **Conformity**: Changing behavior to match group norms.
- **Obedience**: Following orders from authority.
- **Persuasion**: Changing attitudes through influence.
- **Group polarization**: Groups shift toward more extreme positions.
- **In-group**: Group you identify with.
- **Out-group**: Group you see as different from your own.
- **Prejudice**: Negative attitude toward a group.
- **Discrimination**: Behavior that treats people differently based on group membership.
