### Common misconceptions

- Personality determines behavior in all situations.
- If people change across contexts, personality does not exist.
- Traits are labels of moral character rather than descriptive dimensions.
- The five-factor model is a complete description of a person.
- Personality tests are equally accurate regardless of method.
- Self-report inventories always reveal true personality without bias.
- Projective tests reveal hidden truth reliably for everyone.
- Personality is the same as mental disorder diagnosis.
- Psychodynamic theories are either fully scientific truth or fully useless, rather than historically influential with mixed evidence by claim.
- Behavioral theories deny thoughts and feelings rather than focusing on learning history and observable patterns.
- Reciprocal determinism means people control their environment completely rather than interacting with constraints.
