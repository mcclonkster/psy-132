### Common misconceptions

- Culture only matters in special cases and is irrelevant to general psychological science.
- Group averages describe individuals.
- One study generalizes everywhere by default.
- Differences imply deficits or inferiority.
- Ethnocentrism is only intentional prejudice rather than a common interpretive bias.
- Sampling bias is a minor issue if the sample size is large.
- Translation produces identical meaning across contexts without validation.
- Individualism and collectivism fully explain cultural differences.
- Acculturation is simply personal choice rather than shaped by barriers and support.
- Stereotype threat means a group is inherently weaker rather than reflecting context cues and load.
- Cultural competence is only attitude rather than concrete skills.
